El Rastro in Madrid
===================

Planning a visit to Madrid? Then you simply can't miss El Rastro flea market, an iconic open-air market and one of the city's most popular tourist attractions.

**El Rastro in Madrid** is much more than just a street market – it's a chance to find that vintage piece of furniture you’ve been searching for or a unique accessory to take home as a souvenir. You’ll find all kinds of items here, from antiques and second-hand clothing to artwork, all in a truly authentic Madrid atmosphere. Read on for all the details!

**History of El Rastro in Madrid**
----------------------------

El Rastro is one of the oldest markets in the city, with origins dating back to the 18th century. According to historical records, it began around **1740** as a meeting point for the **sale and exchange of second-hand clothing**, serving as an alternative to itinerant trade.

The market was originally located **near the River Manzanares**, close to the **city’s old slaughterhouse**. This is where the name "El Rastro" comes from – as the animals were dragged from the slaughterhouse, they left a trail ("rastro") of blood behind. Over time, the market has retained its unique character and continues to be a cultural landmark for locals.

**Where is El Rastro in Madrid?**
---------------------------------

El Rastro is located in the heart of Madrid, in the **central Embajadores** neighbourhood. This open-air market stretches **across several streets**, with its focal point at **Plaza de Cascorro** and **Ribera de Curtidores****street**.

You’ll find **up to 3,500 stalls** that spread into the nearby Ronda de Toledo and Calle de Embajadores.

**What are El Rastro’s opening hours?**
---------------------------------------

El Rastro is held **every Sunday and on public holidays** throughout the year. It opens **from 9:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m**. It’s a long-standing tradition in Madrid, with many locals visiting regularly.

During the rest of the week, Ribera de Curtidores street is home to many specialised shops, antique dealers, and second-hand bookshops.

**What can you buy at El Rastro market?**
-----------------------------------------

At El Rastro, you’ll find an incredible variety of items for sale, it’s a treasure trove for lovers of **vintage goods and rare finds**.

From vintage furniture to quirky collectibles that will add character to any space, the market offers a wealth of options. The stalls are filled with **antiques, second-hand clothing, old books, and electronic components**. You’ll also come across plenty of one-of-a-kind accessories that you won’t find anywhere else. In short, El Rastro is a true paradise for bargain hunters and those looking for something a bit different.

**Tips for visiting El Rastro**
-------------------------------

A stroll through El Rastro can feel overwhelming due to its size and the number of people it attracts each week. Here are a few tips to help you make the most of your visit:

*   **Try to arrive early**. Arrive ideally when it opens at 9:00 a.m. to beat the crowds and get first pick of the items for sale.
*   **Don’t forget to bring cash**. Many vendors don’t accept card payments.
*   **Don’t be afraid to haggle**. Bargaining is common practice at El Rastro flea market, so feel free to negotiate.
*   **Keep a close eye on your belongings**. As with any busy area in Madrid, pickpockets can be an issue.
