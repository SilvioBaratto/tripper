[](https://www.madrid-tourist-guide.com/en/airport/madrid-airport-transport.html)Madrid Airport Transport To The City Centre
============================================================================================================================

Page Content
------------

This page tells you everything you need to know about Madrid Airport Transport to the city centre. There are options including train, metro, bus, taxi, or car rental, all detailed below, and depending on traffic conditions, they all take more or less 20 - 25 minutes to make the 13 kilometer journey from the airport to the city.

* * *

* * *

* * *

[](https://www.madrid-tourist-guide.com/en/airport/madrid-airport-transport.html)Madrid Airport Taxis
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

![Image 2: Madrid Airport Taxis](https://www.madrid-tourist-guide.com/images/int/airport/madrid-airport3.jpg)

There are 100's of taxis at Madrid airport

**Taxi Tel:**+34 91 321 1000

A taxi is a comfortable and direct way of traveling into to city centre. There are taxis available non-stop, around the clock and you can find them outside T1 (Terminal 1) on the ground floor of the arrivals hall, just in front of gate 2. In T2, also outside the ground floor of arrivals, in front of lounge 5. T3 taxis are on the ground floor, opposite lounge 7 and in T4 are outside the arrivals on the ground floor.

To order a taxi that is especially equipped for disabled access call:

**Euro-Taxi**

**Tel:**+34 91 547 8200

You can call 24 hours a day. Eurotaxi also have taxis equipped for carrying large amounts of luggage. Most of the telephone operators only speak Spanish, so here are some useful phrases:

"Quiero reservar un taxi" - I want to reserve a taxi

 "Para dos personas" - For 2 people

 "Desde el aeropuerto" - From the airport

 "Minusvalidos" - disabled

 "A las tres" - at 3 o'clock

* * *

[](https://www.madrid-tourist-guide.com/en/airport/madrid-airport-transport.html)Taxi Fare
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The journey to the city centre is around 20 - 25 minutes, subject to traffic. In addition, pick-ups from the airport attract a supplement which the driver will add to the displayed fair. The driver should start the meter as soon as you get into the taxi. You can ask for an official receipt and the driver is obliged to provide one.

**Taxi fare:**€33.00

**Additional supplements**

**Monday to Friday from 07:00 - 21:00:**€2.55

**Saturday, Sunday and public holidays from 07:00 - 21:00:**€3.20

**Saturday, Sunday and public holidays from 21:00 - 07:00:**€3.20

**Did you know?** Guide dogs, or any pet in an official pet transport container can travel for free.

* * *

![Image 3: Our Insider Tip](https://www.madrid-tourist-guide.com/images/icon/tip/tip-logo-LANG-EN.png)
Official taxis are white with a red diagonal stripe. I recommend you stick to these taxis as opposed to the unofficial ones, whose drivers will tout for your business inside the airport. The official taxis are all licensed, and the others may not be.

* * *

**[](https://www.madrid-tourist-guide.com/en/airport/madrid-airport-transport.html)EMT Bus**

**Tel:**+34 91 406 8810

EMT is the public bus company that operates throughout Madrid. The 200 bus service runs from T1, T2, T3 to Avda. De América and the service 204 runs from T4 to Avda. De America. The bus services stop in the departures and arrivals of each terminal.

You can travel between any terminal by a free shuttle bus service that runs every 5 minutes.

* * *

![Image 4: Our Insider Tip](https://www.madrid-tourist-guide.com/images/icon/tip/tip-logo-LANG-EN.png)
Children under 4 can travel for free.

* * *

The bus tickets can also be used on the metro system. You can buy a single ticket or a 10 journey 'metrobus' ticket, which saves you money over the 10 journeys.

**Single ticket:**€1.50

**Metrobus ticket:**€12.20

**Operating hours:**06:00 - 23:30

[Official airport website to view Madrid airport transport timetable information](https://www.aena.es/en/adolfo-suarez-madrid-barajas/airport-services/transport-between-terminals.html)

* * *

**[](https://www.madrid-tourist-guide.com/en/airport/madrid-airport-transport.html)![Image 5: metro](https://www.madrid-tourist-guide.com/images/icon/metro/metro.gif)Metro** (underground or subway)

The airport is within zone A of the metro. You will have to take metro line 8 that runs from all the airport terminals going to Madrid city centre.

The metro system is cheap, quick and efficient, and an excellent choice of transport if you don't have too many bags. Trains run from 06:00 - 13:30.

If you think you will be using the metro more often during your stay, then I recommend that you buy a 10 journey 'metrobus' ticket. Journey time to the city centre is around 45 minutes, although you will have to change lines to go right to the centre. Line 8 from the airport terminates at Nuevos Ministerios, (a large station not far from the airport) and from here you can change to either lines 6 or 10. You can also change onto the overground train network from here. See the [official Madrid metro map](https://www.metromadrid.es/es) and then select 'Travel in the metro', followed by 'Metro Map' from the drop down menu.

**Metro Ticket Prices**

**Single ticket:**€1.50

**Metrobus ticket:**€12.20

* * *

**[](https://www.madrid-tourist-guide.com/en/airport/madrid-airport-transport.html)**RENFE Train
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Go to the Line C1 of RENFE running which runs from Terminal 4 Madrid-Barajas airport to the city centre. The train stops at Chamartin, Nuevos Ministerios, Atocha, Mendez Avaro, and Principe Pio.

* * *

[](https://www.madrid-tourist-guide.com/en/airport/madrid-airport-transport.html)Car Hire
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

There are four car hire companies with offices in Madrid Airport. You can find them in both Terminal 1 and Terminal 2.

[Contact numbers for car hire at Madrid airport](https://www.madrid-tourist-guide.com/en/airport/madrid-airport-car-hire.html)

* * *

![Image 6: Our Insider Tip](https://www.madrid-tourist-guide.com/images/icon/tip/tip-logo-LANG-EN.png)
If you are spending a lot of time in the city centre, the best way to travel around is on the metro. Parking is quite difficult, and a car is only necessary if you plan to leave Madrid centre and travel elsewhere.

* * *
