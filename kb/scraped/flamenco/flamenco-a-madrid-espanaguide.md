Is it worth seeing a flamenco show in Madrid?
---------------------------------------

Yes! Madrid is one of the best places in Spain to **experience an authentic flamenco show** with top-quality performers. The city is home to several historic flamenco tablaos that attract some of the best talent of the whole country.

### Essential Flamenco – Tablao Torero

[![Image 10: Passionate bailaor at Taberna Flamenca El Cortijo in Madrid](https://www.espanaguide.com/images/ph/top5.png) BUY TICKETS](https://fareharbor.com/embeds/book/essentialflamenco/items/210284/?sheet-uuid=528213b5-f78e-4fbc-a7d9-41dd89b136fa&asn=espanaguide-eur&asn-ref=Espa%C3%B1a%20Guide&language=en&full-items=yes&flow=no)

Passionate flamenco dancer at Teatro Tablao Flamenco Torero

© ESPAÑA GUIDE

**[Teatro Tablao Flamenco Torero](https://www.espanaguide.com/madrid/flamenco-madrid/essential-flamenco-tablao-torero/) is all about flamenco in its purest form**. The Essential Flamenco show takes place in an **old brick basement with an arched ceiling that produces amazing acoustics**. Because of this setting, there is **no need for any amplification** and instead you only hear and feel each [instrument](https://www.espanaguide.com/flamenco/flamenco-instruments/) directly.

It is quite an **experience to feel the vibrations** from the tapping of the stage and the extra realism that comes from **being so up close and personal**. The basement setting harkens back to the foundations of flamenco when it was first performed in small intimate settings in the famous caves of [Sacromonte](https://www.espanaguide.com/granada/sacromonte/) in [Granada](https://www.espanaguide.com/granada/).

![Image 11: Passionate bailaor at Taberna Flamenca El Cortijo in Madrid](https://www.espanaguide.com/images/ph/top5.png)Where to stay? Find out with our curated list of [The Best Hotels in Madrid](https://www.espanaguide.com/madrid/best-hotels-in-madrid/).

We found the Essential Flamenco show to be **very unique** because there is **much more focus on the musicians** when compared to other venues. This is probably due to the rather small stage and the fact that it is pretty obvious that they want to **showcase the full musical spectrum of flamenco** with a room that really allows the audience to enjoy the sound.

![Image 12: Passionate bailaor at Taberna Flamenca El Cortijo in Madrid](https://www.espanaguide.com/images/ph/top5.png)

Torero, one of best flamenco tablaos in Madrid

© ESPAÑA GUIDE

Besides having dancers and a guitarist, the show also featured **a violinist and percussionist on the cajon** (a wooden box popularized in the 70’s by the legendary [Paco de Lucía](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paco_de_Luc%C3%ADa)).

The artistic direction for the Essential Flamenco show is designed by the famous [Juan el Mistela](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qe8V1LiTxm0), who also dances in some of the shows. [Prices range from 39€ to 49€](https://fareharbor.com/embeds/book/essentialflamenco/items/210284/?sheet-uuid=528213b5-f78e-4fbc-a7d9-41dd89b136fa&asn=espanaguide-eur&asn-ref=Espa%C3%B1a%20Guide&language=en&full-items=yes&flow=no) and include a complimentary drink at the bar on arrival.

However, there is no food or drink service during the performance because this establishment does not want any distractions - just **a pure experience between the audience and the performers**.

Address: [Calle de la Cruz 26, 28012 Madrid](https://maps.app.goo.gl/FwZAsf23Vrhs3sLv8)

[GET TICKETS HERE](https://fareharbor.com/embeds/book/essentialflamenco/items/210284/?sheet-uuid=528213b5-f78e-4fbc-a7d9-41dd89b136fa&asn=espanaguide-eur&asn-ref=Espa%C3%B1a%20Guide&language=en&full-items=yes&flow=no)

### Teatro Flamenco Madrid

[![Image 15: Passionate bailaor at Taberna Flamenca El Cortijo in Madrid](https://www.espanaguide.com/images/ph/top5.png) BUY TICKETS](https://teatroflamencomadrid.com/en/?agente=espanaguide)

Mesmerizing dancer with a bata de cola (long-tailed gown)

© ESPAÑA GUIDE

The [Teatro Flamenco Madrid](https://teatroflamencomadrid.com/en/?agente=espanaguide) is another **one of our favorite shows in the city**. The theater is **spacious, but not too big** – you will still feel like you are right up in the action. The front half of the room has small tables for 2-4 people and the back half of the room is made up of 3 rows of auditorium style seating. **Every seat in the house has great views** to the performance.

The Teatro Flamenco has an **above average production quality** with some of the best sound equipment and lighting that we have seen at a flamenco show. The performance consisted of 1 guitarist, 2 male singers, and 3 dancers (2 female and 1 male). We found the **music to be really top notch** with the singers and the guitarist really harmonizing well together.

![Image 16: Passionate bailaor at Taberna Flamenca El Cortijo in Madrid](https://www.espanaguide.com/images/ph/top5.png)

Three dancers at the stage of Teatro Flamenco Madrid

© ESPAÑA GUIDE

The showstopper was without a doubt the **female dancer that donned the stage in a bata de cola (long-tailed gown)**. This spectacular dress takes considerable effort to dance with and is absolutely mesmerizing as the dancer whips it around the stage.

The Teatro Flamenco Madrid **is not a venue that offers dinner**. They do have a ham & cheese plate as well as a bar. You can enjoy these before and during the show, but **once the show starts, the bar closes down**. The theater recommends to arrive 30 minutes early so you can get settled in and relax with a drink before the shows starts.

Overall, **with the quality of the show and [ticket prices starting at 29€](https://teatroflamencomadrid.com/en/?agente=espanaguide), you really can’t go wrong**. And if you’re looking to _make it extra special_, there are enticing **upgrade options** that get you even better seats and added perks.

Address: [Calle del Pez 10, 28004 Madrid](https://maps.app.goo.gl/3qscBeESTP9FQzNm8)

[GET TICKETS HERE](https://teatroflamencomadrid.com/en/?agente=espanaguide)

[![Image 17: Passionate bailaor at Taberna Flamenca El Cortijo in Madrid](https://www.espanaguide.com/images/ph/top5.png) BUY TICKETS](https://fareharbor.com/embeds/book/tablaodelavilla/?button-tags=espanaguide-asn&ref=espanaguide-asn&asn=espanaguide-eur&asn-ref=espanaguide-asn&full-items=yes&language=en)

Live flamenco show at Madrid's Tablao de la Villa

Housed in a _beautifully restored 19 th-century palace-house_, [Tablao de la Villa](https://fareharbor.com/embeds/book/tablaodelavilla/?button-tags=espanaguide-asn&ref=espanaguide-asn&asn=espanaguide-eur&asn-ref=espanaguide-asn&full-items=yes&language=en) is where flamenco feels intimate, raw, and undeniably real. The venue’s _warm lighting, elegant décor, and pitch-perfect acoustics_ set the stage, while the **thoughtfully arranged seating** ensures that every guest has a **clear, up-close view of the performance**—close enough to feel every stomp, strum, and surge of emotion.

![Image 18: Passionate bailaor at Taberna Flamenca El Cortijo in Madrid](https://www.espanaguide.com/images/ph/top5.png)

Traditional flamenco dancer

![Image 19: Passionate bailaor at Taberna Flamenca El Cortijo in Madrid](https://www.espanaguide.com/images/ph/top5.png)

Setting at Tablao de la Villa

With [tickets starting at 46€](https://fareharbor.com/embeds/book/tablaodelavilla/?button-tags=espanaguide-asn&ref=espanaguide-asn&asn=espanaguide-eur&asn-ref=espanaguide-asn&full-items=yes&language=en) (show + drink), guests can experience a _rotating lineup of five to six artists_, changing weekly to bring fresh energy and unpredictability to every performance. **Shows run one to three times daily**, each lasting about an hour.

To complete the experience, **Tablao de la Villa also features an on-site restaurant**, serving traditional Spanish cuisine. During the booking process, you can choose to _pair the show with a set menu or opt for à la carte dining_, making it an evening of great food and even better flamenco.

![Image 21: Passionate bailaor at Taberna Flamenca El Cortijo in Madrid](https://www.espanaguide.com/images/ph/top5.png)

### Corral de la Morería

[![Image 22: Passionate bailaor at Taberna Flamenca El Cortijo in Madrid](https://www.espanaguide.com/images/ph/top5.png) BUY TICKETS](https://tp.media/r?marker=369924.MadridFlamencoEN_CorralMoreria_PhotoBtn&trs=176088&p=1922&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.viator.com%2Ftours%2FMadrid%2FFlamenco-Show-at-Corral-de-la-Moreria-in-Madrid%2Fd566-6646MORERIA&campaign_id=47)

Stage at Corral de la Morería

C. Lucas Abreu © Madrid Destino

[Corral de la Morería](https://tp.media/r?marker=369924.MadridFlamencoEN_CorralMoreria_TextLink&trs=176088&p=1922&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.viator.com%2Ftours%2FMadrid%2FFlamenco-Show-at-Corral-de-la-Moreria-in-Madrid%2Fd566-6646MORERIA&campaign_id=47) is **one of the most prominent [flamenco tablaos](https://www.espanaguide.com/flamenco/flamenco-tablaos/) in Madrid** and has a history that goes back more than half a century. Throughout the years, it has been visited by a **long list of famous people including Hollywood actors and politicians**.

With that being said, it is also **one of the most expensive shows in the city**. The question is, is it worth it or is it any better than other tablaos? Is it all hype or does it actually deliver?

The answer depends on who you ask and your luck. There is no doubt that **the performers are world class and put on a fantastic show**. However, if you are unlucky, you might get seated in the back or behind one of the many huge wooden columns. And if that happens, it doesn’t really matter how great the show is if you can’t see well.

![Image 23: Passionate bailaor at Taberna Flamenca El Cortijo in Madrid](https://www.espanaguide.com/images/ph/top5.png)

Intense flamenco dance at Corral de la Morería

**The restaurant at Corral de la Morería boasts a Michelin star** which is quite extraordinary and not what one would expect from a dinner theater. But there are many reviews that complain about the same thing – food being undercooked or cold.

[Ticket prices start at 109€](https://tp.media/r?marker=369924.MadridFlamencoEN_CorralMoreria_TextLink&trs=176088&p=1922&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.viator.com%2Ftours%2FMadrid%2FFlamenco-Show-at-Corral-de-la-Moreria-in-Madrid%2Fd566-6646MORERIA&campaign_id=47) which include the flamenco show and a full three-course Michelin star dinner. Upgraded tickets are also available and include a four or five course meal.

Address: [Calle de la Morería 17, 28005 Madrid](https://maps.app.goo.gl/tiwQA8LMBhP4KUNj8)

[GET TICKETS HERE](https://tp.media/r?marker=369924.MadridFlamencoEN_CorralMoreria_Btn&trs=176088&p=1922&u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.viator.com%2Ftours%2FMadrid%2FFlamenco-Show-at-Corral-de-la-Moreria-in-Madrid%2Fd566-6646MORERIA&campaign_id=47)

### Tablao Las Tablas

[![Image 24: Passionate bailaor at Taberna Flamenca El Cortijo in Madrid](https://www.espanaguide.com/images/ph/top5.png) BUY TICKETS](https://www.getyourguide.com/madrid-l46/madrid-flamenco-show-at-tablao-las-tablas--t291775/?partner_id=NDFSZDS&cmp=MadridFlamencoEN_TablaoLasTablas_PhotoBtn)

Bailaora with long-tailed gown

© Tablao Las Tablas

What to know before you visit a flamenco tablao
-----------------------------------------------

#### How long is a flamenco show?

Almost all **flamenco performances last 1 hour**. However, it is best to **arrive a little earlier**. Many venues actually recommend to arrive 30 minutes before the show. That way you can get your drinks or even begin with your meal.

Arriving early can also help you to get a better seat. Although not all venues operate like this, many of them will give **better seats based on a first come, first serve basis**.

#### Can you take photos or videos during a flamenco show?

Most tablaos in Madrid will **allow for photos or short videos** during the show. However, please remember **not to use flash at any time** because it is a huge distraction to the performers and the audience.

#### Is it better to see flamenco in Madrid, Barcelona or Seville?

If we had to choose, we would much prefer to **see a flamenco show in either Madrid or [Seville](https://www.espanaguide.com/seville/) – skip Barcelona**.

Flamenco originates from Andalusia in the south of Spain and so naturally, Seville is a hotspot for talent. But Madrid has its own flamenco scene that is very active and also highly recommended.

Which flamenco show is the best in Madrid?
------------------------------------------

We can’t say that there is one definitive flamenco show in [Madrid](https://www.espanaguide.com/madrid/things-to-do-in-madrid/) that is the best since **there are so many really good ones**. Each venue offers a completely different experience.

Some have _better lighting and sound_ than others. Some have a _larger stage_ that can accommodate more dancers or let them move around much more freely. Some offer _drinks and meals_ during the show while others do not as to avoid any unnecessary distraction.

Ultimately, **deciding which venue to choose comes down to the experience that you think will fit best for you**. The other big factor of course is the _price of the ticket_.
