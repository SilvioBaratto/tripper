The Main Site

Sabatini Building and Nouvel Building

The Museo’s main site has two access points, the Sabatini Building and Nouvel Building, both of which are the starting point of your visit and enable you to familiarise yourself with the different floors and exhibition rooms. [Location and access](https://www.museoreinasofia.es/visit/main-site#getting-here).

It is recommended entering via the Nouvel Building (C/Ronda de Atocha, 2) if you have already purchased your ticket online.

[More information about architectural heritage](https://www.museoreinasofia.es/en/museo/architectural-heritage "The Museo Reina Sofía's Architectural Heritage").

Visits during free opening times are only for **individuals.**

**Free of charge days**

April 18, May 18, October 12 and December 6.

**Public Holidays**

The Museo is closed on 1 and 6 January, 1 May, 15 May*, 9 November*, and 24, 25 and 31 December. *These days may vary depending on the Community of Madrid’s business calendar.

* * *

The **busiest times for visitors** are usually in the morning, between 10 am and 12 pm, and when free access begins at 7 pm.

If you prefer a quieter visit, consider coming to the Museum between 2 and 6 pm.

**Please select the visiting time when purchasing your ticket**; between 10am and the start of free access times, all tickets are assigned a specific time slot for visits.

* * *

You can buy tickets for the museum [online](https://entradas.museoreinasofia.es/en) or at the ticket office. Last entry is 30 minutes before closing time, when the ticket office will also shut.

Remember that your ticket is personal and non-transferable and has an assigned visiting time slot (except during free admission).Please keep it with you throughout your visit as it allows you to remain in the Museo from the time of your visit and to leave and re-enter.

The [garden](https://www.museoreinasofia.es/en/visit/garden-rooftop-terraces "Garden and Rooftop Terraces (Visit)") (currently closed) and [terraces](https://www.museoreinasofia.es/en/visit/garden-rooftop-terraces "Garden and Rooftop Terraces (Visit)") of the main building are free to enter but do not provide access to the rest of the museum.

Some temporary exhibitions may require a ticket with a designated date and time slot, to ensure a quality experience. Show this ticket to museum staff at the entrance to the exhibition.

Retiro Park Galleries
---------------

Palacio de Cristal and Palacio de Velázquez

The Palaces are exhibition spaces located inside Retiro Park. Both venues have been used for installations that make the most of the uniqueness of these spaces.

* * *

The Museo Reina Sofía galleries in the Retiro Park are closed temporarily for architectural renovations. However, you can visit [the installation that covers the Palacio de Cristal](https://www.museoreinasofia.es/en/exhibition/miguel-angel-tornero).
