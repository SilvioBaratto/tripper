#### ALL PRACTICAL INFORMATION ABOUT ACCESS AND VISITING CONDITIONS AT THE MUSEUM

Below is a list of frequently asked questions regarding the appropriate use of our facilities, which aim to help preserve the cultural heritage in our care and to ensure a safe environment for all visitors.

What items are allowed during a visit to the Museum?

*   Handbags with dimensions not exceeding 40 x 30 x 10 cm, provided they are used for their intended purpose and worn at the front of the body.
*   Cameras (use of flash or tripods is not allowed).
*   Small foldable umbrellas (less than 30 cm), which must remain closed throughout the visit.
*   Baby strollers.
*   Any other item whose use or presence is duly justified and that complies with the Museum’s safety and visitor policies.

What items are not permitted?

*   Suitcases, large backpacks, or bulky objects are not allowed inside the Museum. They may not be left in lockers either.
*   Lockers are limited and may be occupied; it is therefore recommended to avoid bringing belongings that require storage. Backpacks exceeding 50 cm on their longest side are not permitted in lockers.
*   Objects or substances that may pose a risk to the safety of people or museum property (e.g., sharp objects, knives, scissors, tools, etc.).
*   Large or pointed umbrellas.
*   Food or beverages (various dining options and designated areas for consumption are available, but not inside rooms containing artwork).
*   Water bottles, except for certified tour guides performing their duties, pregnant women, individuals with illnesses or special needs, or other duly justified cases.
*   Toys, scooters, balls, or entertainment items.
*   Any other object considered by security staff to be a risk to the artwork or to people.

Can I enter the Museum with animals?

*   For safety reasons, pets are not allowed. Only assistance dogs, in accordance with Law 2/2015 of March 10, and emotional support dogs (with proper justification) are permitted.

Recommendations

*   Heritage preservation is a shared responsibility, and we kindly ask visitors to avoid any behavior that may endanger people or artworks.
*   It is important not to touch the artworks at any time and to maintain a safe distance from them.
*   To ensure a pleasant experience for all, children under 14 must be supervised by an adult at all times.
*   Visitors must always follow the instructions given by Museum staff. Failure to comply may result in denial of entry or expulsion from the premises, in which case ticket refunds will not be granted.
