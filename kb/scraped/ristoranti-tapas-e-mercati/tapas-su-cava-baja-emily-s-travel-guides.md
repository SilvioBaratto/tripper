Did you know there is a famous tapas street in Madrid?

Madrid’s Tapas Street is called _Calle de la Cava Baja_, and it’s where you need to go for the best tapas in the city.

Calle Cava Baja is found in La Latina neighbourhood, the best area for tapas in Madrid and one of [the best barrios to stay in](https://emilystravelguides.com/best-areas-to-stay-madrid-2022).

The long narrow street, lined with tapas bars and restaurants, is just a few minutes’ walk from Plaza Mayor in the centre of Madrid.

[![Image 3](https://creative.prf.hn/source/camref:1100lw5tR/creativeref:1100l102651)](https://prf.hn/click/camref:1100lw5tR/creativeref:1100l102651)
Visiting this street is one of the [best things to do in Madrid](https://emilystravelguides.com/madrid-best-things-to-do/), popping in and out of bars for a cold caña and a couple of pinchos.

Keep reading for the best tapas bars on Calle Cava Baja (Madrid’s Tapas Street).

1. Taberna LA CONCHA
--------------

![Image 4: tapas stew taberna la concha calle cava baja](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/best-tapas-la-latina-taberna-la-concha-edited.jpg)![Image 5: tapas taberna la concha la latina tapas street madrid](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/best-tapas-taberna-la-concha-calle-cava-baja-edited.jpg)

_Tapas at Taberna La Concha via [yumcat on Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/yumcat/)_

[Taberna La Concha](http://www.laconchataberna.com/) is the best tapas bar on Madrid’s tapas street, offering local dishes made to share.

Highlights from the menu include _‘albóndigas caseras’_(homemade meatballs), _‘pimientos de piquillo’_(oven-baked stuffed red peppers) and _‘tosta de bacalao’_(traditional smoked cod on crusty bread).

Taberna La Concha also does the best homemade vermouth in Madrid.

![Image 6: taberna la concha entrance calle cava baja](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/taberna-la-concha-calle-cava-baja.png)![Image 7: homemade vermouth taberna la concha calle cava baja](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/homemade-vermouth-taberna-la-concha.jpg)

2. La Perejila
--------------

![Image 8: tostas la perejila calle cava baja tapas street madrid](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/tostas-la-perejila-calle-cava-baja.png)![Image 9: shop front la perejila calle cava baja](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/best-tapas-madrid-la-perejila-calle-cava-baja.jpg)

_Tostas at La Perejila via [hbgrams on Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/hbgrams/?hl=en-gb)_

[La Perejila](https://www.instagram.com/laperejilataberna/?hl=en-gb) is a lively flamenco-inspired tapas bar on Madrid’s tapas street.

The tapas menu here is varied and offers something for everyone.

3. Taberna El Tempranillo
-------------------------

![Image 10: artichoke tapas taberna el tempranillo tapas street madrid](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/tapas-taberna-el-tempranillo-madrid.jpg)![Image 11: tapas taberna el tempranillo calle cava baja](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/tapas-and-wine-taberna-el-tempranillo-calle-cava-baja-edited.jpg)

_Tapas at Taberna El Tempranillo via [kerrysandoeats on Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/kerrysandoeats/)_

[Taberna El Tempranillo](https://m.facebook.com/profile.php?id=137536646301656) is a great little bar with authentic Spanish tapas on offer.

Favourite tapas dishes at [Taberna El Tempranillo](https://m.facebook.com/profile.php?id=137536646301656) include ‘_gambas al ajillo_‘ (sizzling garlic prawns), mushroom pinchos and tuna steak salad.

If you’re mega hungry, both tourists and locals rave about the duck dishes on the main menu.

Tall racks of wine bottles promise the perfect glass of Spanish wine for every palate, and the atmosphere here is buzzing every day of the week.

4. Casa Lucas
-------------

![Image 12: albondigas casa lucas calle cava baja tapas street ](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/tapas-casa-lucas-calle-cava-baja.jpg)![Image 13: croquetas calle cava baja madrid casa lucas](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/tapas-casa-lucas-la-latina.jpg)

_Tapas at Casa Lucas via [her.lifest on Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/her.lifest/)_

[Casa Lucas](https://casalucas.es/) is another great tapas bar on Calle Cava Baja.

Offering really good _albóndigas_ (meatballs) and _croquetas_, it’s popular with both tourists and Madrileños.

The pinchos at Casa Lucas are also tasty, as are the Spanish wines and cold local beers.

5. Díaz y Larrouy
-----------------

![Image 14: tosta and wine diaz y larrouy madrid](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/tosta-diaz-y-larrouy-calle-cava-baja.jpg)![Image 15: tapas menu diaz y larrouy calle cava baja](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/tapas-menu-diaz-y-larrouy-la-latina.jpg)

_Tapas and wine at Diaz y Larrouy via [nikbronder on Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/nikbronder/)_

Diaz y Larrouy is a wine bar with local tapas to accompany the drinks.

The intimate spot offers a variety of tostas if you just want a small snack (their speciality is Canarian cheese with honey – it’s so good!).

Larger dishes from the main menu include peppers stuffed with hake and prawns, Russian salad or melted provolone cheese with tomato and dipping bread.

There’s an extensive Spanish wine selection at Diaz y Larrouy, and they can all be purchased by the glass.

6. Taberna del Tuerto
---------------------

![Image 16: beef cheek tapas taberna del tuerto calle cava baja tapas street](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/beef-cheek-taberna-del-tuerto-calle-cava-baja.jpg)![Image 17: la taberna del tuerto madrid](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/taberna-del-tuerto-calle-cava-baja.jpg)

_Traditional beef cheek via [Taberna Del Tuerto](https://www.instagram.com/latabernadeltuerto/)_

Taberna del Tuerto is a buzzing tapas bar with the best cava in Madrid.

Small _tostas_ are served for free with each drink you order, topped with local ingredients like iberian ham, manchego cheese or pâté.

If you want something a bit more substantial, the _tapas del día_ is always tasty and features dishes like traditional Spanish beef cheek, mini hamburgers and cheese boards.

7. Lamiak
---------

![Image 18: tapas lamiak calle cava baja](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/tapas-and-wine-lamiak-calle-cava-baja.jpg)![Image 19: artichoke hearts calle cava baja madrid](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/tapas-lamiak-calle-cava-baja.png)

_Tapas on Calle Cava Baja via [Lamiak](https://www.instagram.com/lamiakcavabaja/?hl=es)_

[Lamiak](https://lamiak.es/) is a well-loved tapas bar, where stools are clustered around old beer barrels and punters spill out onto the street with a cold beer in hand.

The tapas dishes here are cheap but tasty.

The artichoke hearts with salsa roja are really popular, as is the baked aubergine with mince meat and the vegetarian pintxos.

8. Pez Tortilla
---------------

![Image 20: homemade tortilla pez tortilla madrid](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/chistorra-tortilla-pez-calle-cava-baja.jpg)![Image 21: classic tortilla pez tortilla tapas street madrid](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/tortilla-pez-tortilla-madrid-beer.png)

_Traditional Spanish tortilla in Madrid via [Pez Tortilla](https://www.instagram.com/peztortilla/?hl=en)_

We all know tortilla is typically Spanish, and Calle Cava Baja has its own cantina which is completely dedicated to the dish.

Using the traditional recipes of the founder’s _abuela_, [Pez Tortilla](https://www.instagram.com/peztortilla/?hl=en) offers a selection of homemade tortillas by the slice with crusty bread.

You can’t go wrong with the _clásico_, the authentic recipe with egg, potato and caramelised onion.

Other options include red pepper and morcilla (Spanish black pudding), spicy jalapeño, and goats cheese with spinach.

Tortilla is served warm and in the traditional way with the eggs still slightly runny.

Don’t knock it until you’ve tried it, because it’s absolutely delicious.

9. La Osita
-----------

![Image 22: la osita public house calle cava baja](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/la-osita-craft-beer-cale-cava-baja.jpg)![Image 23: la osita tap room calle cava baja la latina](https://emilystravelguides.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/la-osita-tap-room-calle-cava-baja.jpg)

_The tap room on Calle Cava Baja via [La Osita](https://osobrewco.com/pages/la-osita)_

[La Osita](https://osobrewco.com/pages/la-osita) is technically a tap room rather than a tapas bar, but they serve great little tapas alongside the beer.

Connected to the renowned Oso Brewing, one of Madrid’s best craft beer breweries, La Osita is the place to sample all of the beers on offer in a relaxed atmosphere.

Drinks come accompanied with a tapas of chorizo/ salchichón and sourdough bread.

If you want to order something bigger from the menu, they offer meat and cheese boards.
