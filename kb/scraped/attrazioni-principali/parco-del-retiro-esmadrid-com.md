Information

* * *

_PLEASE NOTE: El Retiro and eight of the city’s other parks (El Capricho, Sabatini Gardens, the Rose Garden in Oeste Park, Juan Carlos I, Juan Pablo II, Quinta de Fuente del Berro, Quinta de los Molinos and Quinta Torre Arias) may close if weather conditions are severe. On these occasions you are also advised not to visit Dehesa de la Villa and Oeste Park which have no gates and cannot be closed to the public._

* * *

**Covering over 125 hectares and comprising more than 15,000 trees, El Retiro Park–recently named a UNESCO World Heritage Site–is a green oasis in the heart of the city. In it you’ll find all kinds of interesting monuments and gardens, including Jardín de Vivaces, Jardines de Cecilio Rodríguez (Andalusian-inspired classicistic gardens), Jardines del Arquitecto Herrero Palacios, the recently-restored[Cat Mountain](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/tourist-information/montana-gatos), the [Rosaleda rose garden](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/tourist-information/la-rosaleda-de-el-retiro/) and Parterre Francés, which holds a Mexican conifer that is nearly 400 years old and is believed to be Madrid’s oldest tree.**

In addition to its role as one of the city’s green lungs, it is also a popular spot among Madrileños who like to go there for a stroll, to do some sport, visit an exhibition or take the kids to a puppet show. The park is home to a large artificial **[lake](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/tourist-information/estanque-grande-de-el-retiro/)**, where you can rent a rowing boat, and to the **[Velázquez Palace](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/tourist-information/palacio-de-velazquez/)** and [**Glass Palace**](http://www.esmadrid.com/en/tourist-information/palacio-de-cristal/) which are both currently used as exhibition halls by the **[Reina Sofía Museum](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/tourist-information/reina-sofia-museum)**. The latter is a beautiful glass pavilion built in 1887 to house exotic plants for an exhibition on the Philippines. It is one the finest examples of cast-iron architecture in Spain.

![Image 18: Montaña de Los Gatos](https://estaticos.esmadrid.com/cdn/farfuture/1oeBIJ-NbszYm7zqPReVx77wHDYH2Ucxa854m3huhjc/mtime:1690562017/sites/default/files/styles/content_type_full/public/recursosturisticos/infoturistica/montana_de_los_gatos2.jpg?itok=5nRJRkwh)

El Retiro is also filled with interesting sculptures and fountains such as the magnificent [**Monument to Alfonso XII**](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/tourist-information/monumento-alfonso-xii), which watches over the lake and in spring of 2018 opened to the public an observation deck offering wonderful vistas. Near the Rose Garden, you’ll encounter the statue of the **[Fallen Angel](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/tourist-information/el-angel-caido/)**, the only sculpture in the world dedicated to the devil which curiously enough sits 666m above sea level. Other spots worth visiting are the **[Galápagos Fountain](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/tourist-information/fuente-de-los-galapagos-o-fuente-de-isabel-ii/)**,built in honour of the then princess Isabella II, the [**Teatro de Títeres**](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/tourist-information/teatro-de-titeres-de-el-retiro), the only theatre in Europe that stages puppet shows every weekend, and the large area known as **Reservado de Fernando VII**which King Ferdinand VII decided to keep for himself and his family when the rest of the park was opened to the public. In this section of El Retiro,between Calle O’Donnell and Calle Menéndez Pelayo, you’ll find some of the king's "whims", small buildings or monuments designed as little retreats for the monarchs to rest and relax: **Casa del Pescador**, the recently reopend **[Cat Mountain](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/tourist-information/montana-gatos)** (it had been closed to the public for twenty years)and **Casa del Contrabandista**, which now houses [**Florida Park**](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/restaurants/florida-park), a former nightclub which has been turned into a multi-purpose space serving top-notch food and hosting shows and other events.![Image 19: Foso de los monos](https://estaticos.esmadrid.com/cdn/farfuture/ai8xUlFWMGtNrxNDw5CmTF97pCairUSUYjyBOdjAmJM/mtime:1698672186/sites/default/files/styles/width_650/public/styles/large/public/foso_de_los_monos.jpg?itok=aIfT-qnf)

Since autumn 2023, visitors to El Retiro Park can also explore the fully restored **Monkey Pit**, which is part of what used to be the Retiro Menagerie, the precursor to [**Madrid Zoo**](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/tourist-information/zoo-aquarium). For a century and a half, it was home to dozens of baboons, and today, it remains as a decorative display that recalls that iconic zoo. To enjoy it up close, you can sign up to a guided tour organized by the park.

**A wonderful green space where you can relax... or work out**

A firm favourite with both locals and tourists, El Retiro is a great place to go rollerblading, for a long walk or a jog. Dotted with playgrounds of different sizes, it’s just as popular with the little ones.

As you explore El Retiro, you could also visit the **Municipal Sports Centre La Chopera**, the **Eugenio Trías​ Public Library**, the **Casa de Vacas Cultural Centre** or the recently renovated area surrounding the **Meteorological Observatory** (one of the historic sites within the Park, which has a private area, the Castillo del Telégrafo gardens, and a pool known as El Baño de la Elefanta, where elephants once bathed).

And if you are passionate about botany, be sure to visit the **ahuehuete or Montezuma cypress**, perhaps one of the oldest trees in Madrid. It is said to have stood on this spot since 1630 and it is a very exotic species that does not lose all its leaves every year, just a few of them. Apparently, during the War of Independence against the French, it was one of the few specimens that were saved in the Park, which was taken over by the French as their headquarters. Apparently![Image 20: Parque del Retiro - Olivo Centenario](https://estaticos.esmadrid.com/cdn/farfuture/DQkLJKxESmwF4tMhZ4Buf_ACl9zIlYLY3_3hGLGPBjA/mtime:1681371950/sites/default/files/styles/width_650/public/styles/large/public/olivo_retiro.jpg?itok=soP6oEQe), Napoleon’s soldiers decided to set up a huge cannon between the branches of the enormous tree and fired them from there.

However, the oldest tree in El Retiro is currently a **627-year-old olive tree** dating from approximately 1396, planted in one of the meadows near the Gate of the Fallen Angel.

If you fancy pedaling around the park, you can **[rent a bicycle](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/bike-rental-in-madrid)**from one of the bike rentals in the area. You’ll also find a number of outdoor cafés and kiosks dotted around El Retiro where you can get a drink or bite to eat.

Every year the park plays host to popular events such as the [**Book Fair**](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/whats-on/ferialibromadrid-parque-de-el-retiro) and the fireworks display held in honour of **[San Isidro](http://www.esmadrid.com/en/whats-on/sanisidro-madrid),** the city’s patron saint.

**A UNESCO World Heritage Site**

On 25 July 2021, El Retiro Park and the **[Paseo del Prado](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/tourist-information/paseo-prado)**, known jointly as the Landscape of Light, [were declared a **UNESCO World Heritage Site**](https://www.madrid.es/portales/munimadrid/es/Inicio/Actualidad/Noticias/El-Paisaje-de-la-Luz-de-Madrid-es-desde-hoy-Patrimonio-Mundial/?vgnextfmt=default&vgnextoid=e06a27173fcda710VgnVCM1000001d4a900aRCRD&vgnextchannel=a12149fa40ec9410VgnVCM100000171f5a0aRCRD).In this extraordinary site that boasts “outstanding universal value”, culture, science and nature have coalesced since the mid 16 th century. The area is home to such landmarks as the Prado, Thyssen-Bornemisza and Reina Sofía museums, Cibeles Palace **–**the current seat of Madrid City Council**–**, Puerta de Alcalá gate and the Botanical Gardens.
